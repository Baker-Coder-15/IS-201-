Meal Prep & Simple Strength Programs

Fuel your training, don’t overcomplicate it.

by Jake Fullmer • Updated December 2025

1. Beginner Push–Pull–Legs Split

This is a simple three-day split built for busy students. You can run it three days a week (e.g., Mon–Wed–Fri) and repeat it for 8–12 weeks, adding weight slowly as you get stronger.

Day 1 – Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

  • Barbell or Dumbbell Bench Press – 3 × 6–8
  • Incline Dumbbell Press – 3 × 8–10
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press – 3 × 8–10
  • Lateral Raises – 3 × 12–15
  • Tricep Dips or Pushdowns – 3 × 10–12

Day 2 – Pull (Back, Biceps)

  • Pull-ups or Lat Pulldown – 3 × 6–10
  • Barbell or DB Rows – 3 × 8–10
  • Single-Arm Dumbbell Row – 3 × 8 each side
  • Face Pulls – 3 × 12–15
  • Bicep Curls – 3 × 10–12

Day 3 – Legs (Quads, Hamstrings, Glutes)

  • Back or Front Squats – 3 × 6–8
  • Romanian Deadlifts – 3 × 8–10
  • Walking Lunges – 3 × 10 steps each leg
  • Leg Curls or Hamstring Sliders – 3 × 12
  • Calf Raises – 3 × 15–20

Start lighter than you think you need, move with control, and add weight only when your form is solid. The goal is to build a base, not to ego lift.

2. High-Protein Meal Prep Ideas

These are simple meals that reheat well, travel easily, and hit your protein without destroying your budget. Adjust portion sizes based on your own goals.

Chicken, Rice, & Veggie Bowls

  • 4–5 oz grilled or baked chicken breast
  • 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • Mixed vegetables (frozen works great)
  • Optional: salsa, hot sauce, or teriyaki

Cook your chicken and rice in bulk once or twice a week, then build bowls in containers. Add sauce right before eating so things don’t get soggy.

Ground Beef Taco Mix

  • 93/7 or 96/4 lean ground beef or turkey
  • Taco seasoning
  • Rice or tortillas
  • Shredded lettuce, salsa, cheese (optional)

Use this mix for bowls, tacos, or nachos. It’s flexible and keeps well in the fridge for a few days.

Overnight Oats

  • Rolled oats
  • Greek yogurt
  • Milk or almond milk
  • Fruit and a little honey
  • Optional: protein powder or nut butter

Mix everything in a jar the night before, then grab it in the morning. It’s quick, cheap, and surprisingly filling.

3. Recovery, Mindset, & Mental Health

Training is more than sets and reps. Your sleep, stress levels, and mental health matter just as much as your workout plan.

  • Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep when you can.
  • Build a simple cooldown routine with light stretching or breathing.
  • Use training as a way to process stress, not to run from it.
  • Remember that consistency beats perfection. Missing a day doesn’t ruin anything.

The goal is to build a life you can actually sustain: strong body, clear mind, and space to grow spiritually, mentally, and physically.
